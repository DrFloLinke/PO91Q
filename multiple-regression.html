Interpreting a coefficient

The order in which to interpret a coefficient is as follows:

  1. Is it significant? If not, all you can say is that there is no influence. There is insufficient evidence to support the alternative hypothesis.
  2. If it is significant, you can interpret its size and direction according to the statistical model (for example, slope coefficient vs. partial slope coefficient).
  3. What does the coefficient mean for the hypothesis? Look at the direction. Is the direction as predicted by the hypothesis? Then you have evidence to support the hypothesis. If the direction is inverse, then you have falsified the hypothesis, even though you have a significant coefficient.
